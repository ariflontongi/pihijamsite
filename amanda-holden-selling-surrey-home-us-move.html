    The Britain's Got Talent judge could be moving stateside

      Amanda Holden's £5million beloved Surrey home is now back on the market, as she makes a determined effort to sell it.

      At first, it was believed that the The Britain's Got Talent judge could be looking for another UK home, or spending more time at her second property in the Cotswolds, but it transpires that the star could be destined for a stateside move.

      Is Amanda Holden moving to the US?

      The Sun reports that Amanda is considering a US relocation and she has spent weeks meeting with US producers as well as the America's Got Talent team. This comes after Amanda's daughter Lexi's desire to go to university across the pond has been revealed.

      It is well known that Amanda has a close relationship with friend and colleague Simon Cowell, and he could be encouraging the life change for Amanda by helping her with stateside contacts.

      Amanda Holden's Surrey home she's giving up

      Amanda and her husband Chris Hughes are hoping to make a £1.5million profit on the home, which they purchased for £3.5m in 2015.

      When the couple bought their home, the TV judge excitedly told fans: "So happy!! Just completed on our forever home! #lovemyhubby our girls are sooo excited!"

      The property features a modern kitchen, a lavish bedroom, a beautiful dressing room, a spacious dining area and a huge garden space. There is even a private bar inside the house – how luxurious?

      Amanda also recently added a luxury outhouse to the Surrey property featuring a second bar and a hot tub.

      Despite the impressive features, Amanda maintains that it is not a "show home". Speaking to House Beautiful, the mum-of-two said: "We have two little girls and don't want them feeling that they're treading on eggshells."
