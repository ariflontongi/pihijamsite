Today, Buddhist monks no longer patch their robes together from discarded cloth; they use donated or purchased cloth, according to Buddhism Zone. While they no longer use plant-based dyes, they still retain traditional color. In the Theravada Buddhism tradition, monks continue to wear bright orange or saffron robes to emulate the Buddha. Not just anyone can wear a saffron robe; they are reserved for ordained monks only. To Theravada Buddhists in Thailand, the color represents the light shining in darkness, according to Buddhism Zone.
