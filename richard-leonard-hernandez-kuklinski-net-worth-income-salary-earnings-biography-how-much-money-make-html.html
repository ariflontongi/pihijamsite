    Richard Leonard, often known as “The Iceman,” Kuklinski (April 11, 1935 – March 5, 2006) was an American serial killer who was convicted of three murders. He was born in New York City and raised in Pennsylvania. Because of his technique of freezing a victim in order to conceal the moment of death, Kuklinski was given the moniker “Iceman.”  Prior to his arrest, Kuklinski, who stands at 6’5″ (196 cm) and weighs 300 pounds (135 kg), resided with his wife and children in the New Jersey town of Dumont. His family was oblivious to Kuklinski’s double life and criminal activities, according to reports. After Kuklinski was arrested on suspicion of two murders, the New Jersey Police Department established a task team to investigate him.  His arrest was the result of a year-long undercover operation. Kuklinski claimed to be a contract assassin for the DeCavalcante criminal family in Newark and the Five Families of the American Mafia in New York City, according to authorities. Following his convictions for murder, Kuklinski participated in a series of interviews, during which he claimed to have killed somewhere between 100 and 250 individuals (his “recollections” fluctuated) between 1948 and 1986, according to his claims.
