    Long before Gabriel Guilbeau was cast as Chef Gator on Yellowstone, the California native was working as an actual chef on the set of the show. But in a delicious twist of fate, Gator was soon plucked from the catering line to become a character on the show.

    As the personal chef to the Dutton family (helmed by patriarch Kevin Costner), Gator is known for his cajun-inspired meals and homestyle food. Now, the real-life Gator is cooking up some new recipes inspired by the show for Yellowstone: The Official Dutton Ranch Family Cookbook.

    Related Stories

    Normally priced at $35, but on sale right now for $21 at Amazon (a 40 percent savings), the Yellowstone cookbook is published by Simon & Schuster and features more than 55 recipes that are certainly not vegan or made from wheatgrass.

    The Yellowstone cookbook is also available as an e-book for Kindle e-readers and the Kindle mobile app for $13.99

    Inside the cookbook, you’ll find simple and tasty homestyle dishes, including Rip’s Fry Bread with Scrambled Eggs and Bacon, Beth’s Cheesy Hamburger Mac Casserole, Rancher’s Chicken and Biscuit Dumplings, and much more. It includes recipes for boozy beverages, like Beth’s “Two Scoops of Ice Cream, Three Shots of Vodka” Smoothie. The cookbook features handy tips for the kitchen, such as how to make the perfect smoked pulled pork, how to make biscuits exceptionally fluffy, and more.

    In addition, there’s a gift set available, which includes the cookbook with more than 45 recipes, an adjustable high-quality Yellowstone-branded apron, and more. While it contains fewer recipes than the standalone cookbook, the addition of the apron makes it worth giving as a gift — especially for the Yellowstone fan in your life. The gift set retails for $40, but it’s on sale for $36, or 10 percent off, at Amazon.

    Meanwhile, after airing its midseason finale back in Jan. 2023, Paramount announced that Yellowstone will return for the second part of season five, which includes its series finale, sometime in 2024. The studio announced two new spinoff series tentatively called 1944 and 2024 that will continue the saga of the Dutton family tree.
